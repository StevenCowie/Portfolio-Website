Octopus Game

Throughout my time at university we had to do various solo assignments. We were tasked with making a game and getting it to work with an Arduino controller. My controller was a simple joystick. I did all the programming for the game, and even made a moving sprite for the octopus.

VR Development

For this module we were tasked with making a VR game using different sorts of haptics. My game was pretty simple yet intuitive. In one hand you hold your sword (the controller) which vibrated every time you hit an enemy and in the other hand you held a dragon egg (vive tracker). You had to walk from one side of the room to the other without moving the dragon egg too quickly or it would break, all while enemies were spawning around you.

Squid Vs Eel

This was one of the very first projects I worked on at univeristy. It was done in a team of 4 programmers. The game is a 1v1 between a squid and an eel, both of which have different abilities in which you must learn how to use efficiently. It was created in Python using PyGame.
